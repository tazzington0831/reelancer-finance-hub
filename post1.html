Top 5 Budgeting Apps for Freelancers in 2025

Staying on top of your finances as a freelancer is challenging. Luckily, there are plenty of budgeting apps designed to simplify tracking income and expenses. Below is a round‑up of our favorite tools and why they stand out.

1. YNAB (You Need A Budget)

YNAB uses a zero‑based budgeting system that encourages you to assign every dollar a job. It’s perfect for irregular incomes because you budget based on money you already have rather than forecasting future income.

Key features: Goal tracking, real‑time sync, educational resources.

2. Mint

Mint aggregates your bank accounts, credit cards and bills to give you a complete overview of your finances. It categorizes transactions automatically and alerts you when you exceed your budget.

Key features: Bill reminders, credit score monitoring, customizable budgets.

3. PocketGuard

PocketGuard simplifies budgeting by calculating how much you can safely spend after accounting for bills, savings and goals. It’s ideal for freelancers who need a quick snapshot of their spending power.

Key features: In‑my‑pocket figure, subscription tracker, effortless categorization.

4. EveryDollar

EveryDollar follows a zero‑budgeting approach similar to YNAB but with a more streamlined interface. The free version allows manual transaction entry, while the paid plan imports transactions automatically.

Key features: Easy‑to‑use interface, debt snowball tool, Dave Ramsey’s budgeting methods.

5. Goodbudget

Goodbudget uses envelope budgeting to allocate money into digital envelopes for various categories. It’s great for couples or partners who share finances.

Key features: Virtual envelopes, debt tracking, multi‑device sync.

Choosing the Right App

The best app for you depends on your preferences and needs. If you want robust tracking and educational resources, YNAB is a solid choice. For a free all‑in‑one overview, Mint is hard to beat. PocketGuard is ideal for a quick glance at your spending power. EveryDollar offers a simple interface with a paid upgrade for automation, and Goodbudget helps those who like the envelope method.
